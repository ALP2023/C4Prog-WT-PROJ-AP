About Me

My initial foray into computer programming was back in the dark ages as a 1st year Computer Science student. It was back when you needed to remember DOS commands to use a PC (Macs were new to the market and exorbitantly expensive)! I have foggy memories of programming in Pascal and BASIC, but what I do clearly remember was that I excelled at writing buggy code. Needless to say that was the end of my career as a programmer, and the beginning of my career as a geoscientist. It's a much better fit as it aligned with my strong desire to travel and connect with the natural environment.

However, this early coding experience has stood me in good staid, as I continued to tinker with snippets of codes, such as manipulating Excel macros, VB for Word documents, and SQL scripting when playing with database back ends. It's the last of this skill set that I wanted to expand, which led me to do the Cert IV programming course at NMT. I now routinely flex my newly developed SQL scripting skills in my current job, and now have C#, Python and Web design in my tool box. Who said you couldn't teach an old dog new tricks?
