Resumé

April Pickard

Dr. Pickard boasts an extensive career spanning over 20 years across the resources industry and research. Her rich expertise encompasses diverse geological realms, having worked in Australia, South East Asia, the North Sea (Norwegian sector), and South Africa. Proficient in mineral exploration, database administration, developing workflows and protocols, seismic interpretation, basin reviews, and field mapping. She excels in database management, facilitating seamless processing, interpretation, and retrieval of geological data. Her technical proficiency extends to various database platforms and geological 2D/ 3D visualisation packages.

Her career trajectory illustrates versatile roles, from serving as a reservoir geologist in the Oil and Gas sector to contributing as a technical research geoscientist with CSIRO, showcasing her adaptability and resourcefulness. Dr. Pickard's skill set encompasses an array of disciplines, from sedimentology to isotope geochemistry, petrology, and ore geology. Her roles demanded not just technical acumen but also effective collaboration, establishing robust relationships within interdisciplinary teams. Whether collaborating internationally or within local settings, she demonstrated adeptness both as a team member and leader. A hallmark of her expertise lies in her adeptness at bridging intricate technical capabilities with high-level geoscientific research, enabling the synthesis of disparate pieces of evidence into comprehensive studies, evidenced by her contributions to publications in esteemed journals including Nature (1997). This skill was critical in her contributions to various projects, including seismic interpretation, database development, and her pivotal role in diverse sampling programs within the offshore Bight Basin.
